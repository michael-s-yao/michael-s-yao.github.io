Surgical resection with adjuvant cisplatin and osimertinib dual chemotherapy

47 y.o. M with PMHx of HTN, T2DM (on metformin, A1c 6.2%), and HLD non-smoker with persistent cough, chest pain, and 15 lb unintentional weight loss f/t/h newly diagnosed Stage IIB NSCLC adenocarcinoma.

According to their chart, the patient underwent genetic testing and was EGFR positive and PD-L1 negative with high Ki-67 expression. Recent CT imaging from 10/21 demonstrated a unilateral R pulmonary mass that appears to be surgically resectable.

Adjuvant osimertinib after complete resection has been shown to substantially improve outcomes for EGFR-mutant stage IB–IIIA disease. This is the standard adjuvant targeted option for sensitizing EGFR mutations [1].

Separately, adjuvant cisplatin-based chemotherapy remains standard-of-care for stage II adenocarcinomas and improves survival after resection [2]. Given high Ki-67 proliferative index—which is associated with worse prognosis and higher recurrence risk in recent meta-analyses—adding cytotoxic adjuvant chemo is reasonable to reduce early systemic relapse risk [3].

[1] Wu YL, et al. Osimertinib in resected EGFR-mutated non-small-cell lung cancer. NEJM. (2020).

[2] Kenmotsu H, et al. Randomized Phase III study of pemetrexed plus cisplatin versus vinorelbine plus cisplatin for completely resected Stage II to IIIA nonsquamous non-small-cell lung cancer. J Clin Oncol. (2020).

[3] Wang D, et al. Prognostic value of Ki-67 expression in advanced lung squamous cell carcinoma patients treated with chemotherapy. Cancer Manag Res. (2021).
